# Campaign Article Template

Follow this structure when generating a Campaign Article. The tone should be more energetic and playful than Product Explainers while keeping campaign mechanics crystal clear.

---

## Structure

```
**Meta description:** [Under 160 characters. Include campaign name. Create urgency or excitement.]

# [Campaign Name]: [Catchy subtitle — sentence case]

[Opening hook: 1–2 sentences. Energetic, creates excitement or urgency. Can reference the prize pool, a unique mechanic, or a time-limited opportunity.]

[Bridge: 1–2 sentences. Briefly state what the campaign is and why it matters to the reader.]

## What is [Campaign Name]?

[2–3 paragraphs covering:]
- The campaign concept and theme
- The total prize pool or reward structure
- The campaign period
- Why this campaign is worth participating in

## Campaign details

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Campaign period | [Start date] – [End date] (UTC) |
| Eligible users | [New users / Existing users / All / Specific criteria] |
| Total prize pool | [Amount and currency, e.g., "50,000 USDT"] |
| KYC required | [Yes, Level X / No] |
| Eligible regions | [Global / Specific regions / Exclusions] |

## How to participate

[Brief intro: 1 sentence. E.g., "Getting in is simple — here's what to do."]

### Step 1: [Action — e.g., "Sign up or log in"]

[1–2 paragraphs. Clear instructions. Include any registration links or codes if applicable.]

### Step 2: [Action — e.g., "Complete KYC verification"]

[1–2 paragraphs. If KYC is required, be specific about which level.]

### Step 3: [Action — e.g., "Make your first deposit"]

[1–2 paragraphs. Specify minimum amounts, eligible currencies, etc.]

### Step 4: [Action — e.g., "Start trading to earn rewards"]

[1–2 paragraphs. Explain the core campaign mechanic.]

[Add more steps as needed]

## Prizes and rewards

[Detailed breakdown. Use a table if there are multiple tiers:]

| Tier | Requirement | Reward |
|------|-------------|--------|
| [Tier 1] | [What to do] | [What you get] |
| [Tier 2] | [What to do] | [What you get] |
| [Tier 3] | [What to do] | [What you get] |

[1–2 paragraphs explaining:]
- When rewards are distributed
- How winners are selected (if applicable)
- Any caps or limits

## Terms and conditions

- [Key condition 1 — e.g., "Participants must complete Level 1 KYC before the campaign end date"]
- [Key condition 2 — e.g., "Sub-accounts and institutional accounts are not eligible"]
- [Key condition 3 — e.g., "Bybit reserves the right to disqualify accounts engaged in wash trading"]
- [Key condition 4 — e.g., "Rewards will be distributed within 14 business days after the campaign ends"]
- [Add more as needed]

[Note: Link to full T&C if available. E.g., "For the complete terms and conditions, visit [Campaign Page]."]

## [Start/Join/Don't miss out on] [Campaign Name]

[1–2 sentence closing. Playful, direct, urgent. Restate the key benefit or prize.]

[CTA — e.g., "Join the campaign now and start earning your share of the 50,000 USDT prize pool."]
```

---

## Guidelines

- **Word count**: 800–1,200 words (campaigns are shorter and punchier)
- **Tone**: Witty and playful in the intro and CTA. Precise and clear in mechanics/T&C sections.
- **Dates**: Always include timezone (UTC). Use Bybit house format.
- **Amounts**: Always include token symbol. Use commas for 1,000+.
- **Urgency**: Reference the campaign end date or limited spots if applicable.
- **Clarity on mechanics**: The "How to participate" section is the most important. A reader who only skims this section should understand exactly what to do.
- **T&C**: Always include this section. Even if the user's brief doesn't provide full T&C, include the most common conditions (KYC, sub-accounts, wash trading, distribution timeline).
- **No overpromising**: Don't guarantee returns or specific outcomes. Use "up to" for prize pools.
