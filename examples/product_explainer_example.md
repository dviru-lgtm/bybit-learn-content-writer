# How to get started with Bybit Copy Trading

You've heard that crypto trading can be profitable, but every time you open a chart, it looks like a foreign language. Candlesticks, support levels, RSI — where do you even begin?

What if you could skip the steep learning curve and let an experienced trader make the calls for you? That's exactly what Bybit Copy Trading does. It connects you with top-performing traders on the platform and automatically mirrors their trades in your account — no chart-reading required.

## Key takeaways

- Bybit Copy Trading lets you automatically replicate trades from experienced traders with just a few taps.
- You stay in control: set your own investment amount, pick your traders and stop copying at any time.
- Copy Trading is a learning tool, not a guaranteed profit machine — always manage your risk.

## What is Bybit Copy Trading?

Bybit Copy Trading is a feature that lets you link your trading account to one or more expert traders (called "Master Traders" on Bybit). Once you're connected, every trade a Master Trader opens or closes is copied into your account automatically, scaled to the amount you've chosen to invest.

Think of it like following a recipe from a professional chef. You don't need to know why they added a pinch of salt at that exact moment — you just follow along and get a solid result. Over time, you can watch what they do and start learning the reasoning behind each move.

Here's how it works at a high level:

1. **Master Traders** share their positions publicly on Bybit. Each trader has a profile showing their win rate, total return, drawdown and trading style.
2. **Followers (you)** browse Master Trader profiles, pick the ones that match your risk appetite and click "Copy."
3. **Automatic execution** — when a Master Trader opens a position, the same trade opens in your account. When they close, you close. The system handles everything in real time.

Copy Trading on Bybit supports both spot and derivatives markets, so you can copy trades across different asset types depending on what the Master Trader focuses on.

### Who is Copy Trading for?

Copy Trading is built for people who want market exposure without spending hours learning technical analysis. It's especially useful if you:

- Are new to crypto and want to learn by watching experienced traders in action.
- Have limited time to monitor the market but still want to participate.
- Want to diversify by following multiple traders with different strategies.
- Prefer a hands-off approach but still want more control than a managed fund.

That said, Copy Trading isn't a "set it and forget it" money printer. Markets are unpredictable, and even the best traders have losing streaks. You should still check in on your portfolio regularly and adjust if something isn't working.

### Copy Trading vs. trading on your own

If you're wondering whether Copy Trading is right for you or whether you should just learn to trade independently, here's a quick comparison.

| | Copy Trading | Trading on your own |
|---|---|---|
| **Time commitment** | Low — check in weekly | High — daily chart analysis |
| **Knowledge needed** | Basic understanding of risk | Technical and fundamental analysis |
| **Control** | Partial — you set limits but don't pick entries | Full — you make every decision |
| **Learning curve** | Gentle — you learn by observing | Steep — trial and error |
| **Risk management** | Shared with the Master Trader's decisions | Entirely up to you |

Many traders start with Copy Trading to get comfortable with how the market moves and then gradually transition to placing their own trades as their confidence grows. There's no rule that says you have to pick one or the other — you can copy trade and trade independently at the same time.

### How fees work

Bybit keeps the fee structure simple. There are no extra charges to use Copy Trading itself. You pay the standard trading fees on each copied trade (the same fees you'd pay if you placed the trade yourself). If a Master Trader generates a profit for you, they earn a profit-sharing percentage — typically 10% of the net gains. This is deducted automatically, so you don't need to calculate anything manually.

For example, if a Master Trader's copied trade earns you 50 USDT in profit and their profit-sharing rate is 10%, they receive 5 USDT and you keep 45 USDT.

## How to start Copy Trading on Bybit

Getting set up takes less than five minutes. Follow these steps to start copying your first trader.

### Step 1: Open the Copy Trading page

Log in to your Bybit account (or create one if you haven't already). From the top navigation, hover over **Trade** and select **Copy Trading** from the dropdown menu.

[Screenshot: Bybit top navigation bar with the Trade menu expanded showing the Copy Trading option highlighted]

### Step 2: Browse Master Traders

You'll land on the Copy Trading dashboard, which shows a list of Master Traders ranked by performance. Use the filters at the top to sort by metrics that matter to you — total return, win rate, follower count or trading style (spot vs. derivatives).

Take your time here. Look for traders with:

- A track record of at least 30 days.
- Consistent returns rather than one huge spike.
- A maximum drawdown you're comfortable with (lower is safer).

[Screenshot: Copy Trading dashboard showing the Master Trader leaderboard with filter options and key stats like ROI, win rate and follower count]

### Step 3: Review a trader's profile

Click on a Master Trader's name to open their full profile. Here you'll see detailed performance charts, trade history, average holding time and their bio. Pay attention to the **drawdown** figure — it tells you the biggest loss the trader has experienced from a peak, which gives you a sense of the risk involved.

[Screenshot: A Master Trader's profile page showing their performance chart, trade history, drawdown percentage and follower stats]

### Step 4: Set your copy parameters

Once you've found a trader you like, click **Copy**. A settings panel will appear where you can configure:

- **Investment amount** — the total amount (in USDT) you want to allocate to this trader.
- **Per-order amount** — the fixed amount used for each copied trade, or choose "Fixed ratio" to mirror the trader's position size proportionally.
- **Take-profit / Stop-loss** — optional limits that close your copied position automatically if it hits a certain profit or loss threshold.
- **Max position size** — caps the total amount at risk at any time.

Start with a smaller amount while you get a feel for the trader's style. You can always increase it later.

[Screenshot: Copy settings panel showing fields for investment amount, per-order amount, take-profit and stop-loss with a "Copy" confirmation button]

### Step 5: Monitor and manage your copies

After you tap **Copy**, you're live. Head to the **My Copy Trading** tab to track all your active copied positions, profit and loss and the Master Traders you're following. From here you can:

- Pause or stop copying a specific trader at any time.
- Adjust your investment amount or risk settings.
- Close an individual copied position manually if you disagree with the trade.

[Screenshot: My Copy Trading dashboard showing active copied positions, cumulative P&L, and management options like "Stop Copying" and "Adjust" next to each Master Trader]

## Tips for getting the most out of Copy Trading

**Don't put everything on one trader.** Diversify across two to four Master Traders with different strategies. If one hits a rough patch, the others can help balance your overall performance.

**Start small and scale up.** Begin with 100 USDT to 200 USDT per trader until you've seen how they perform over a few weeks. Once you trust their style and risk management, you can increase your allocation.

**Set a stop-loss on every copy.** Even the best traders have bad days. A stop-loss protects you from a single trade wiping out weeks of gains. A common starting point is a 20-25% stop-loss on your total invested amount per trader.

**Check in weekly, not hourly.** Copy Trading works best when you give it time. Obsessively watching every trade creates unnecessary stress. Set a weekly reminder to review performance and make adjustments if needed.

## FAQ

### Is Copy Trading on Bybit free?

There's no additional fee to use the Copy Trading feature. You pay the same trading fees as any regular trade on Bybit. The only extra cost is the profit-sharing percentage that goes to the Master Trader when they make you money — typically around 10% of net profits.

### Can I lose money with Copy Trading?

Yes. Copy Trading mirrors real trades, and all trading carries risk. If a Master Trader takes a losing position, you take that loss too (proportional to your investment). That's why it's important to set stop-losses, diversify across multiple traders and only invest money you can afford to lose.

### Can I stop copying a trader at any time?

Absolutely. You can stop copying any Master Trader whenever you want from the My Copy Trading dashboard. When you stop, you can choose to close all open positions immediately or let existing trades play out until the trader closes them. There's no lock-up period or penalty for stopping.
